## Full Description

The Franklin OnChain U.S. Government Money Fund (FOBXX) is a SEC-registered money market fund managed by Franklin Templeton Trust that invests in U.S. government securities. It is notable as the world's first U.S.-registered money market fund deployed onchain. The fund tokenizes its shares as BENJI tokens, with 1 share of FOBXX equaling 1 BENJI token. The fund's transfer agent maintains the official record of share ownership via the proprietary Benji platform, which utilizes public blockchain networks for transaction activity. Yield accrues daily and is distributed to shareholders through newly minted BENJI tokens.

The fund targets both retail and institutional investors, though availability varies by chain. On Stellar, BENJI is available to retail and institutional investors, while on Polygon, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Aptos, Ethereum, Base, Solana, and Canton, it is restricted to institutional investors. All participants require KYC/AML verification to mint, redeem, and in most cases transfer or hold the token, consistent with its status as a regulated investment product.

BENJI differentiates itself from stablecoin-based RWA products by representing direct shares in a registered money market fund rather than a synthetic or wrapper structure. The fund leverages blockchain technology for record-keeping efficiency while maintaining the regulatory framework of a traditional mutual fund. Franklin Templeton also issues iBENJI and gBENJI/sgBENJI variants tracked separately. The smart contract code has been audited by Trail of Bits and Ancilia, providing third-party security assurance.

- The SEC EDGAR entry for Franklin Templeton Trust (CIK 0001786958) shows that the company's Exchange Act registration has been revoked and its Municipal Advisor registration has been revoked and cancelled. This applies to the trust entity's Exchange Act filings; the fund itself remains registered as an investment company under the Investment Company Act.
- BENJI is deployed across 9 chains (Stellar, Polygon, Arbitrum, Avalanche, Aptos, Ethereum, Base, Solana, Canton). The DeFiLlama notes indicate the tracked row also includes iBENJI fund-token contracts on Ethereum and BNB Smart Chain, while gBENJI and sgBENJI are tracked separately.
- The SEC EDGAR page for this entity shows 158 filings since September 3, 2019, but no recent 8-K, 10-K, 10-Q, or proxy filings in the last 365 days, and no insider transaction or beneficial ownership filings. The entity's fiscal year ends March 31.
- A specific launch date for the BENJI token product could not be determined from the available sources. Franklin Templeton announced the Benji platform expansion to Canton, but no public contract address was listed at the time of the DeFiLlama data.
